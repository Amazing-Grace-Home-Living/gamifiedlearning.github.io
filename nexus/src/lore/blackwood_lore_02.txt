THE BLACKWOOD CHRONICLE — SCROLL II

Beyond the archive, the Whispering Pines stand sentinel
at the border of the known world.

Seven stones, carved by hands no longer remembered, form a ring
around the oldest pine. At dusk, when light slants between the trunks,
shadows fall across the stones in a pattern that mirrors the night sky.

The grove remembers what was spoken within it.
Walk quietly. Ask a question. Wait.

Those who have listened report hearing, beneath the wind,
a voice that does not belong to any creature of the forest —
older than pine, older than stone.

The second star echoes through the pines whenever a true question is asked.
